What are the Big 5 Traits?

Neuroticism

Neuroticism measures susceptibility to negative emotion. Someone high in trait neuroticism is more likely to suffer from anxiety, anger issues, depression, destructive self-consciousness, inability to indulge in moderation, (food or TV binges) and/or an increased sense of vulnerability.

Extroversion

Extroversion measures outward interest. A very extroverted person tends to direct their thoughts and actions outwards and typically towards others. Being high in extroversion can be characterized by friendliness, sociability, assertiveness, high activity levels, a desire for excitement, and general cheerfulness.

Openness

Openness is sometimes referred to as "openness to experience." Openness measures one's ability to understand and grapple with new ideas. High openness is marked by a vivid imagination, artistic interest, intense emotion, desire to experience new things, intellect, and, more generally, liberalism.

Agreeableness

Agreeableness measures a tendency to avoid conflict and behave sympathetically. Someone high in agreeableness is likely to be trustworthy and also place trust in others. They tend to act morally, care about others, work well in a team, and show great humility.

Conscientiousness

Conscientiousness measures carefulness and vigilance. High conscientiousness is manifested in orderliness, a sense of duty and responsibility, self-discipline, and a strong desire for achievment. Conscientious people typically take an organized approach when confronted with tasks, and can be more likey to complete them successfully.
