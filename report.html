Each trait falls somewhere between 1 and 5, with 1 being the lowest possible trait value and 5 being the highest. For example, someone with an Agreeableness trait of 4.5 might be considered very agreeable, whereas someone with an agreeableness trait of 2 might be considered fairly disagreeable.

A quick note: percentile ranks compare you to others in the population. For example, a percentile rank of 70 in agreeableness means you are more agreeable than 70% of the population.

Neuroticism measures susceptibility to negative emotion. Someone high in trait neuroticism is more likely to suffer from anxiety, anger issues, depression, destructive self-consciousness, inability to indulge in moderation, (food or TV binges) and/or an increased sense of vulnerability.

Extroversion measures outward interest. A very extroverted person tends to direct their thoughts and actions towards others and naturally gravitates toward social environments. Being high in extroversion can be characterized by friendliness, sociability, assertiveness, high activity levels, a desire for excitement, and general cheerfulness.

Openness is sometimes referred to as "openness to experience." Openness measures one's ability to understand and grapple with new ideas. High openness is marked by a vivid imagination, artistic interest, intense emotion, desire to experience new things, intellect, and, more generally, liberalism.

Agreeableness measures a tendency to avoid conflict and behave sympathetically. Someone high in agreeableness is likely to be trustworthy and also place trust in others. They tend to act morally, care about others, work well in a team, and show great humility.

Conscientiousness measures carefulness and vigilance. High conscientiousness is manifested in orderliness, a sense of duty and responsibility, self-discipline, and a strong desire for achievment.
